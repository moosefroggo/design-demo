# **The Challenge**

We're looking for someone with **strong taste** - not just technical design skills. This challenge is designed to help you showcase **clear, opinionated standards for what a good learning experience should feel like, and that you can execute on them.

This challenge is reviewed on a **first-come, first-reviewed basis**, with a recommended completion window of ~1 week. We ask that you read through the challenge carefully, then communicate with Natasha you’re estimated time of delivery. We want to understand how you communicate your progress on large pieces of work that have no specified deadline. 

---

## **Part 1: UX Improvement**

**Task:** Pick **one element of the NextWork learning experience** that you think can be improved right now. Then build a functioning prototype of your improved version.

Possible areas you could explore (but not limited to):

- Completing a **task -** there’s so much to unpack here! Some learners like to do tasks, some don’t. Some want an easier task, some want a greater challenge. Some want to add their own additional tasks to record troubleshooting steps they’ve done, or an addition where they’ve gone “off-script” from a project to build something epic. How can we make tasks more dynamic and magical?
    
    ![image.png](Product%20Designer%20Challenge/image.png)
    
- **Code Blocks** - understanding code blocks is a constant challenge for our learners. How could this be better?
    
    ![image.png](Product%20Designer%20Challenge/image%201.png)
    
- **Sharing** your work - when a learner share’s their portfolio, it’s a huge win for NextWork. How can this be an even more magical experience?
    
    ![Screenshot 2026-05-04 at 8.55.00 AM.png](Product%20Designer%20Challenge/Screenshot_2026-05-04_at_8.55.00_AM.png)
    

**Deliverable:**

A redesigned element of NextWork. This part must include:

1. A working prototype link of the redesigned component or interaction. Use Figma, code, whatever you like!
2. A Loom video **or** 1-page written walkthrough covering: what you changed, why, what details add delight to the learner experience, and what you'd ship next

**What we're looking for:**

- Your design taste and your ability to identify a real problem and improve it with conviction. We want to see *why* you chose what you chose, and *how* your version is better.
- Your prototype should replicate the design and feel of the current NextWork platform as much as possible. This way, it is easier to understand what is the incremental improvement that you’re trying to showcase!
- We highly recommend using the NextWork platform first to truly understand a learner’s experience.

## **Part 2 - Motion Design**

**Task:**

Create **x2 Rive animations** that add delight to the learner experience. You decide:

- **What kind of animation** it is (celebration, transition, feedback, idle state, etc.)
- **Where it lives** in the platform (some recommended areas to explore are unlocking a Secret Mission, completing a project by clicking Mission Accomplished, loading state, onboarding, etc.)

**Deliverable:**

- x2 completed Rive animation files, with a brief video for each animation showing when and where the learner would encounter the animation, and what feeling it creates.

**What we're looking for:**

This part carries significant weight. We're looking for your motion sensibility - does the animation feel native to a learning context? Does it add genuine delight without being distracting? You’re welcome to use other motion software outside of Rive.

---

### **Part 3 - Portfolio Themes**

**Task:**

NextWork auto-generates portfolio websites for learners. Right now there are **3 themes: Light, Dark, and Fun.** 

![image.png](Product%20Designer%20Challenge/image%202.png)

Design **2-3 additional portfolio themes** and present your vision for them. Design themes that are beautiful and scroll-stopping. The goal is for a learner to share their portfolio and have someone reading the post stop, click, and remember it.

**Deliverable**

Each theme needs:

- A live web page design
- Shareable assets (the download PNG card, preview, the OpenGraph when a learner posts on LinkedIn or X). *Hint: Look for what learners are already sharing about NextWork on LinkedIn. How could you make it more visually appealing?*
- Clear reasoning in video or written format that explains your decisions and the learner profile that this would attract. What kind of learner picks this theme, and why?

**What we're looking for:**

Range and taste. Can you design for different learner personalities and aesthetics while keeping the product coherent? We want to see that you understand who NextWork's learners are and can design themes they'd actually be proud to share. Make it memorable!

## **❓ Frequently Asked Questions**

---

**Q: Can I use AI tools to help with the challenge?**

Yes - NextWork is an AI-first team. Jon (our founding designer) uses Claude Code extensively in his own workflow. What we're evaluating is your taste and judgment, not whether you built everything from scratch.

---

**Q: Is there a hard deadline for the challenge?**

No fixed deadline - the timeline is flexible. We recommend ~1 week, as the process is first-come, first-reviewed and things are moving quickly.

---

**Q: Can I reach out with questions while I'm working on the challenge?**

Yes - please do. Check-ins and updates throughout the process are a positive signal and can help you succeed. We don’t recommend keeping quiet about any doubts and questions!

Book 15 mins with Natasha [here.](https://calendar.app.google/MZqNWj8uSu2koPba6)

---

**Q: Does this role require me to write code?**

Not strictly - but you will need to be comfortable executing in tools like Claude Code. The role is design-first, and strong taste, execution in motion and AI prototyping tools, plus a fit with the NextWork culture is what matters most. 

---

**Q: What is your interview process for the Product Design role?**

1. Design challenge (you are here📍)
2. Meet [Amber](https://www.linkedin.com/in/amber-joseph/) (our founder and CEO)
3. Meet [Jon](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jonneylon/) (our founding designer)
4. Meet the rest of the team
5. Questionnaire 👀
6. Full in-person day at Austin HQ (flights are on us, if you’re out of Texas)
7. OFFER 🤙🏻
